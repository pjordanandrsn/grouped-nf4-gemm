**Box:** Latitude.sh `g3-h100-small` (DAL), bare metal. NVIDIA H100 PCIe 80 GB, driver

triton 3.7.1, bnb 0.49.2 with `BNB_CUDA_VERSION=130` (`versions.txt`).

## 1. Reducer defect (J1): the summary printed `None` over intact evidence

The run's original `SUMMARY.txt` reads, verbatim:

```
flagA_fused.json -> None
flagA_none.json -> None
flagB_real.json -> 7.100403757011133
```

…followed in the run log by `AB-DONE — evidence complete`. **Diagnosis: the JSONs are
intact and were mis-parsed by the reducer** (the preferred outcome — no cell was lost,
no re-run needed). The inline summary read key `tok_per_s`; the harness writes
`toks_per_s`. Its fallback then read `waterfall_toks`, which (a) does not exist in the
Phase-A schema (`waterfall_ceiling_toks`) → `None` ×2, and (b) *does* exist in the
Phase-B schema → the one non-`None` line printed the **ceiling** (7.100) as if it were
the achieved rate (3.924). The same typo disabled the per-cell J/token lines.

**Fix (forward-only):** [`ab_reduce.py`](ab_reduce.py) is now the canonical reducer —
schema-aware, and under the **CELL-VOID rule** (AB-VOID applied one layer down): a cell
that produces no parseable row emits `CELL-VOID <file> reason=…` and the reducer exits
non-zero; a summary may never print `None` as a value while declaring evidence
complete. Negative-tested on truncated and schema-mismatched JSON. The original
`SUMMARY.txt`/`ENERGY.txt` are preserved verbatim in the receipts (receipts are never
repaired); the corrected reductions are the separate files `SUMMARY_reduced.txt` /
`ENERGY_reduced.txt`, regenerated by the committed reducer.

## 3. The additive law (J3): retire the ratio, promote `t_token ≈ c_box + bytes/L`

Two hosts (phaseB5 at 45 GB/s, desktop L40S at 27 GB/s) read 0.77× of waterfall and the
desktop doc called the fraction "the box-invariant." The gen5 box reads **0.553×** with
a *correct, greedy-identical* kernel. The fraction was never the law — the additive
form, registered as PROJECTIONS Addendum-1's serialization term, is. Per-box constant
recomputed from every stamped real-decode receipt, `c = 1/med_off − B/L` with
B = 7.9839 GB/token from each receipt:

| host | receipt | link L (GB/s) | med off (tok/s) | t_token (ms) | B/L (ms) | c_box (ms) | fraction |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| RunPod H100 SXM (phaseB2) | `phaseB2.json` | 45.45 | 4.303 | 232.4 | 175.7 | **56.7** | 0.756 |
| RunPod H100 SXM (phaseB3) | `phaseB3.json` | 55.09 | 4.331 | 230.9 | 144.9 | **86.0** | 0.628 |
| RunPod H100 SXM (phaseB4) | `phaseB4.json` | 51.78 | 4.351 | 229.8 | 154.2 | **75.7** | 0.671 |
| RunPod H100 SXM (phaseB5, stamped headline) | `phaseB5.json` | 44.98 | 4.330 | 231.0 | 177.5 | **53.5** | 0.768 |
| DO H200 (replication) | `do_replication/phaseB_do.json` | 55.35 | 4.427 | 225.9 | 144.2 | **81.6** | 0.639 |
| Desktop-class L40S, gen4 ×16 | `receipts-desktop-20260721/flagB_real.json` | 26.96 | 2.607 | 383.5 | 296.1 | **87.4** | 0.772 |
| **Latitude H100 PCIe, gen5 bare metal** | `receipts-gen5-20260722/flagB_real.json` | **56.69** | **3.924** | **254.8** | **140.8** | **114.0** | **0.553** |

**The claim, on every surface from now on:** `t_token ≈ c_box + cold_bytes/L`, with
`c_box` **measured at 53.5–114.0 ms across seven hosts** and **not ordered by link
speed** (the slowest link carries a mid-pack c of 87.4; the fastest carries the largest,
114.0; four similar fat pods span 53.5–86.0). It is launch latency + attention +
router serialization + non-expert compute + host effects — genuinely per-box. The
achieved fraction is therefore *derived, not constant*: it falls as L rises because the
transfer term shrinks against a roughly fixed floor. The 0.77 agreement between phaseB5
and the desktop was a coincidence of two hosts whose overheads happened to scale with
their links — and the record already contained 0.63–0.67 fractions (B3, B4, DO) that
the "0.77 constant" reading ignored.

**Addendum-1's open fork is now closed by data.** Addendum-1 said the five fat-host
receipts could not distinguish "per-host serial-path variance" from "flat ~226–232 ms
per-token total" (both fit in-sample). The two new hosts break the tie: per-token
totals of 383.5 ms (desktop) and 254.8 ms (gen5) refute the flat-total reading; the
per-box-constant reading survives and is the model going forward.

**Findings vs. the directive's own table** (every number recomputed from receipts, as
ordered): flagship c = **53.5** ms (not ~51) at L = 44.98 (not 45.2), fraction 0.768
by receipt-median (the stamped doc's "0.771×" corresponds to its best pair of runs,
4.343 tok/s; median-of-six gives 0.768); desktop c = **87.4** (not ~89, same rounding
class); gen5 = 114.0 ✓. **The "A100-PCIe, 26.7 GB/s, 0.77, c≈127" row could not be
recomputed: no A100 235B Phase-B receipt exists in the record.** The only A100 receipts
anywhere are 12-card kernel-census cells (`bench/phase2/arch/sweep_20260715/a100/`,
no offloaded decode), and the interim gen4 energy pod at that link class was an L40S
running Phase-A only. The row is also internally inconsistent (0.77 at 26.7 GB/s
implies c ≈ 89 ms; c = 127 ms implies a 0.70 fraction). The record supplies the seven
hosts above instead.

**Grading against the stamped prediction (pre-registered band, cited as ordered):**
`PROJECTIONS-multiarch.md` Addendum 1 (2026-07-18, OTS-stamped **before this run**)
pre-registered gen5-class real decode at **4.0–5.0 tok/s, falsification <3.6 or >5.5**,
from t_s ∈ [53.5, 86.0]. Measured **3.924: below the band, above the falsification
line** — a narrow miss, not a falsification, and the *reason* is exactly the term the
band's derivation flagged: this box's c (114.0 ms) lies outside the fitted t_s range,
which was estimated from five virtualized fat-host pods and never claimed to bound
bare-metal hosts. The stamped falsification framework worked as designed: the original
pure-waterfall gen5 row (6.0–6.9) **is falsified** by this measurement, as Addendum-1
itself pre-registered it would be. `PROTOCOL-multiarch.md` Amendment 1's pass floor
(`achieved ≥ 0.80 × the Addendum-1 predicted band` → ≥ 3.2 tok/s) **PASSES** at 3.924.
Note also the sibling result: the desktop's 2.60–2.61 sits **inside** Addendum-1's
revised gen4 band (2.4–3.0) — one revised band confirmed, one missed narrowly, both
now superseded by the per-box additive form (a *measured-c* prediction, not a band
from a transferred range). The additive model itself was held-in-advance as Addendum-1's
serialization term with a fitted range; it was **not** stamped with a per-box band for
this host class — the seven-host c table above is a **characterization**, not a
confirmed law.

## 4. c_box direct probe (J4): specified + implemented, first metered run post-gate

c_box above is *inferred by subtraction*. Both harnesses now measure it directly:

- `offload_decode_235b.py --no-stream` (Phase-A synthetic): expert H2D stream disabled,
  event cadence and everything else identical; per-token time IS the pipeline floor.
  Functional smoke on the local A2000 (tiny synthetic config, timing-only): stream-on
  6.9 ms/token vs `C-BOX-PROBE c_box = 5.6 ms/token` — mechanism verified, numbers
  non-citable by design.
- `offload_generate_235b.py --c-box-probe` (Phase-B real loop — the c_box that matters):
  `copy_slot` skips the H2D and keeps id bookkeeping; the off-mode token loop runs
  otherwise unchanged (router, attention, MoE GEMMs on stale staged bytes, launches,
  syncs). Stale-byte logits are garbage, so the probe never breaks on EOS, marks its
  receipt `c_box_probe: true`, and reports `c_box_ms` — the reducer prints it as
  `C-BOX-PROBE`, never as a throughput claim.

This makes `plan_placement()` predictive from **two microbenches** (link rate +
measured floor) and turns the fraction into a printed *output*. **No metered c_box
runs before the gate** (rails); the probe rides the next post-gate box at ~2 min of
wall clock.

## 5. 3.92 vs 4.33 reconciled (J5): the box, quantified — not a regression

Same checkpoint (`Qwen/Qwen3-235B-A22B-Instruct-2507`), same 94 layers, byte-identical
quantize path (`per_token_gb = 7.983857664` in both receipts), same harness lineage and
modes, greedy-identical output gates passed on both. The difference decomposes exactly
under the additive law:

```
phaseB5:  t = 231.0 ms = 53.5 (c) + 177.5 (7.98 GB @ 44.98 GB/s)  -> 4.33 tok/s
gen5:     t = 254.8 ms = 114.0 (c) + 140.8 (7.98 GB @ 56.69 GB/s) -> 3.92 tok/s
                          +60.5 host floor    −36.7 transfer       net +23.8 ms/token
```

The gen5 box bought 36.7 ms/token of transfer with its faster link and spent 60.5
ms/token of it on a larger host floor (16-core EPYC 9124 reporting 58% scaling MHz at
inventory, PCIe host effects — vs the fat-CPU virtualized SXM pods). **Verdict: box
(c_box), not model/config and not a regression.** The two numbers are comparable only
under the additive decomposition; their fractions (0.77 vs 0.55) are not comparable at
all, which is the point of §3. The direct probe (§4) will replace this inference with a
measurement on the next box.
